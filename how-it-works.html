What Tulo Square Is ‚Äî and Is Not

What Tulo Square Provides

  • Digital tools for managing community savings circles (modern chilimba)
  • A platform to connect savings circles with verified local businesses
  • Financial education content for community learning
  • Transparent tracking of contributions and commitments
  • Templates for community agreements and dispute resolution

What Tulo Square Does NOT Do

  • Not a bank: We don't accept deposits or hold funds
  • Not an investment platform: We don't offer securities or investment products
  • Not a financial advisor: We don't give investment advice
  • Not a payment processor: Money moves directly between users via mobile money/banks
  • Not a fund manager: Communities manage their own funds

The Three Pillars of Tulo Square

1. Digital Savings Circles

Create or join digital versions of traditional savings groups (chilimba, stokvel) with people you trust. Set goals, track contributions, and manage payouts transparently.

  • Set clear rules and schedules together
  • Track contributions with digital receipts
  • Automate reminders for contributions
  • View real-time progress toward goals

2. Bridges to Business

Connect your savings circle with vetted local businesses. Support their growth through pre-purchases, group funding, or community commitments.

  • Browse verified business profiles
  • Commit funds as a group to support growth
  • Track how your support impacts businesses
  • Earn community rewards (discounts, early access)

3. Community Learning Hub

Access financial education designed for Zambian communities. Learn about savings, budgeting, supporting businesses, and making informed financial decisions together.

  • Bite-sized lessons on financial topics
  • Case studies from successful community groups
  • Tools for tracking community financial health
  • Guidance on creating fair community agreements

The Tulo Square Bridge in Action

Tulo Square is the bridge that connects community savings with local opportunity, while keeping money and decisions within the community.

Starting Small is Smart

You don't need a large group to begin with Tulo Square. Many users start small to learn the platform and prepare for future community building.

Ways to Start Small

  • Learn First: Complete financial education modules before forming a circle
  • Family Square: Start with immediate family members
  • Interest-Based: Create a Square around a specific goal (school fees, business seed)
  • Digital Chilimba: Modernize an existing savings group

Examples of Square names: Mwaba Family Fund, Kalingalinga School Fees Pool, Kitwe Youth Business Seed

Community Agreements & Transparency

Tulo Square provides templates and guidance for creating fair community agreements, but the agreements are between community members, not with Tulo Square.

Key Agreement Elements

  • Clear contribution amounts and schedules
  • Decision-making processes (unanimous, majority, designated leaders)
  • Dispute resolution procedures
  • Withdrawal conditions and notice periods
  • Record-keeping and transparency requirements

Tulo Square provides the tools ‚Äî communities make the agreements.

Regulatory Perspective
